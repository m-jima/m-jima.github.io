Game of Gale

The Game of Gale, otherwise known as the Shannon switching game, is a two-player connection game invented by American mathematician Claude Shannon. The most commonly played version of the game is played on a rectangular grid (as opposed to an arbitrary graph), invented by American mathematician David Gale in the 1950's.

How to play:

  • Blue goes first.
  • Players take turns connecting two adjacent dots, provided there is no line separating them.
  • A player wins if they can construct a continuous path from the top/bottom or left/right of the board.
Blue wins!
